Senior Robotics Software Engineer

As a surgical robotics software engineer, my primary responsibility is to develop and maintain the software backend that controls a image guided needle targeting robot named ANT-C. I have been working on ANT-C system which integrates with hospital's CT machine to assist doctors with needle procedures. This involves designing and coding the software that controls the movement and functionality of the robot. during needle targeting surgical procedures such as biopsy and ablation.

To verify the accuracy and safety of the system, I have to devise experiments that test the performance of the robot. This involves running simulations and conducting real-world experiments with test jigs to evaluate the robot's accuracy and safety. Through these experiments, I help to identify any issues or areas for improvement in the software or hardware, and work to make adjustments as needed to optimize the performance and safety of the system.


In my role as a surgical robotics software engineer, I had the opportunity to assist doctor in conducting needle targeting tests on a laboratory pig by operating the robot. We use this test to verify the accuracy and safety of the system.

Subsea Mechanical Design Engineer

My role as a subsea mechanical design engineer is to design subsea Christmas trees, which are mechanical assemblies used to regulate oil or gas flow from subsea wells. To achieve this, I create 3D CAD models and drawings, select materials, build the bill of materials, conduct simulations and calculations, oversee the fabrication and assembly processes, and design factory acceptance tests for the assemblies.

After more than a year of design preparation, I was given the opportunity to travel to Montrose, Scotland headquarter where the manufacturing is taking place to give advices and make adjustments to parts and factory acceptance tests.


As a design engineer, I have to regularly resolve NCRs (non-conformance) that arise during the manufacturing process. This involves identifying the root cause of the non-conformance, developing and implementing corrective actions, and ensuring that the corrective actions are effective in preventing similar issues from occurring in the future.


As part of my responsibilities, I am tasked with designing Factory Acceptance Test for subassemblies to the API 6a standards. One of the test conducted was pressurising the inner bore as shown in the photo. The test designs will incorporate measures to verify the subassembly's functionality and to identify any potential problems under testing conditions.


In my role as a design engineer, my primary responsibility is to ensure that my designed parts do not collide with one another and are assembled correctly. I need to be highly familiar with the manufacturing processes and the geometric tolerances to ensure that the final product meets all of the required quality standards.

Furthermore, I have to do calculations to assess the mechanical strength and integrity of the assemblies. We have to ensure the stress points and forces that will be exerted on the assemblies during operation, and conducting simulations and tests to ensure that they are capable of withstanding these forces without experiencing any mechanical failure.


Once the manufacturing, assembly and testing processes are complete, the final subsea Christmas trees were transported on a vessel to the installation site, where it is carefully lowered into the sea and positioned on their respective wellheads.
